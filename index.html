Deal Overview

CoStar Group (NASDAQ: CSGP), the $35B real estate information and analytics company behind Apartments.com and LoopNet, has agreed to acquire Matterport (NASDAQ: MTTR) for $5.50 per share in an all-cash transaction valuing the company at approximately $1.6 billion.

The offer represents a 225% premium to Matterport's undisturbed closing price. Matterport is a spatial data company whose 3D capture technology has digitized over 40 million spaces across 177 countries, creating "digital twins" of physical buildings used in real estate, insurance, construction, and facilities management.

The deal is structured as a one-step merger requiring Matterport shareholder approval and standard regulatory clearances. CoStar will fund the acquisition entirely from its $4.9B cash position — no debt issuance required.

Why It Matters

The data play: CoStar's core business is making commercial real estate searchable and measurable. Matterport's 3D digital twins add a spatial layer to that dataset — turning flat listings into walkable, measurable environments. For institutional property investors and brokers, this is the difference between looking at photos and having floor-by-floor analytics.

The AI angle: Matterport's spatial data library (40M+ scanned spaces) becomes a proprietary training dataset for CoStar's property AI tools. No competitor has anything close to this scale of 3D real estate data. The defensibility isn't in the hardware — it's in the accumulated dataset.

Vertical integration: CoStar has been on an acquisition spree building a full-stack real estate platform (Apartments.com for residential, LoopNet for commercial, Ten-X for auction). Matterport fills the "digital experience" gap — the ability to tour and measure a property without visiting it.

⚠ Risks to Watch

Premium justification: 225% is an eye-watering premium for a company that's never been profitable. Matterport posted -$82M net income in FY2024 on $164M revenue. CoStar is betting on revenue synergies that don't exist yet.

Integration complexity: Matterport's customer base is heavily consumer/prosumer (realtors, small property managers). CoStar's strength is enterprise. Bridging that gap without losing Matterport's existing users is non-trivial.

Hardware dependency: Matterport still generates ~30% of revenue from camera hardware sales, which is low-margin and declining. The strategic value is in the software platform, but the hardware tail creates margin drag during integration.

✓ Bull Case

Data moat: 40M+ digitized spaces is an unreplicable asset. As property analytics moves toward AI-driven valuation and automated inspections, whoever owns the largest spatial dataset wins.

Distribution arbitrage: Matterport has the tech but limited enterprise distribution. CoStar has 300K+ commercial real estate clients who'll get Matterport baked into their existing workflows — zero customer acquisition cost for the highest-value segment.
